Installation

A quick one-time setup to get the app running on your Mac.

ℹ️

Why does macOS warn you about this app? Apple requires developers to pay a $99/year fee to have their apps automatically trusted by macOS. Table Read is in beta and hasn't gone through that program yet, so the first time you open it, macOS will flag it as "unverified" and block it. It's not dangerous. The steps below walk you through telling your Mac it's okay to run it. Once you've done it once, it won't ask again. Future releases after beta will be Apple-certified and install like any other app.

  1. 1
    Open the downloaded TableRead-0.1.0.dmg file.
  2. 2
    Drag Table Read into the Applications folder shortcut in the window.
  3. 3
    Go to your Applications folder and double-click Table Read.
    macOS will show a warning that it can't verify the app. Click Done to dismiss it for now. That's expected.
  4. 4
    Open System Settings → Privacy & Security and scroll toward the bottom. You'll see a message that Table Read was blocked. Click Open Anyway.
  5. 5
    Enter your Mac password or use Touch ID to confirm. Table Read will open, and won't ask again.

What it does

A complete pipeline from PDF to audio, all on your Mac.

🎭

Full cast of voices

Every character gets a distinct voice. macOS system voices are built in with no setup. Kokoro (free, runs locally) and OpenAI TTS are optional upgrades for more natural results.

📄

PDF to audio

Drop in any standard screenplay or play PDF. Table Read parses the characters, dialog, scenes, and stage directions automatically. No special formatting required on your end.

✏️

Editable parse

Review every line before generating. Fix mis-attributed dialog, rename speakers, add or remove lines. Corrections are saved and re-applied automatically the next time you import the same PDF.

🔒

Fully private

Everything runs on your Mac. No data leaves your machine unless you choose to use OpenAI voices, and even then only the dialog text is sent.

How it works

Table Read walks you through four steps: import, review, cast, generate. Each step is designed to give you full control without requiring any technical knowledge.

The whole thing runs locally. You can pause a render, change voices, re-import with corrections applied, and pick up right where you left off.

  • Step 1 Import a script PDF Drag a file in or click Choose PDF. Table Read parses characters, dialog, and scenes in seconds.
  • Step 2 Review & correct Inspect every line. Fix attribution, rename speakers, or remove noise. Corrections persist across re-imports.
  • Step 3 Cast your voices Assign a voice to each character. Mix and match engines per role.
  • Step 4 Generate audio Select scenes and hit Generate. Each scene becomes a standalone .m4a file.
